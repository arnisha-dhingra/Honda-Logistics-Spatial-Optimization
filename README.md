# Logistics & Spatial Optimization Engine
**Honda Cars India Limited | KD Unit**

## Executive Summary
This repository outlines the structural methodology and algorithmic approach developed during my Operations Data Internship at Honda Cars India Ltd. The primary objective was to resolve severe supply chain bottlenecks and maximize warehouse spatial throughput using data-driven modeling and automated operational tools.

*⚠️ **Data Privacy Notice:** Due to strict Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDA) and corporate data security policies, all proprietary facility blueprints, load distribution data, and internal automation source code have been withheld from this public repository. The details below outline the generalized mathematical and structural approach.*

---

## System Architecture & Methodology

### 1. Multi-Variable Load Analysis
* **The Challenge:** Historical logistics mapping relied on static heuristics, leading to hidden spatial inefficiencies and localized operational bottlenecks.
* **The Solution:** Engineered an analytical framework to map high-dimensional load distribution data against physical floor boundaries, isolating overlapping zones and underutilized vertical matrices.

### 2. Automated Space-Utilization Tool
* **The Challenge:** Safety compliance checks and load modeling were manual, high-latency processes.
* **The Solution:** Developed a custom, automated analytical tool (utilizing advanced macro/formula logic) that dynamically calculates spatial allocation. It instantly flagged structural safety violations and suggested optimized stacking permutations.

---

## Business Impact
* **Throughput Maximization:** Successfully executed dynamic vertical stacking and layout optimization strategies to unlock **950+ m² of entirely new warehouse space**.
* **Latency Reduction:** Significantly accelerated the engineering evaluation speed for load safety compliance, transitioning the KD unit from reactive manual checks to automated predictive modeling.
